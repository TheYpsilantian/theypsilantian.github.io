The Encampment Resolution. Again.

Resolution 240 failed 3-3 in October 2024. Peeler happened in January 2026. King and Brown visited the encampment in May 2026. King called for removal on June 3: "It's our responsibility. It's our land." Reports of sexual assault, children in the woods, and fires at the encampment are documented.

The question Tuesday June 16 is whether the vote math has changed. King (YES) and McLean (almost certainly YES) are two. Brown visited with King — hard to vote no. If Brown votes yes: three. Wilcoxen is the swing. Simmons will almost certainly vote no. Fellows likely follows Simmons ideologically.

The Ypsilantian will have a reporter in chambers Tuesday June 16. The Ypsilantian has documented the full context of every vote cast on this issue since October 2024 and every condition at the encampment since the February 2025 vacate notice.

Who We Are and Why We Exist

The Ypsilantian is an independent watchdog publication covering Ypsilanti and Washtenaw County, Michigan. We are named after M.T. Woodruff's original Ypsilantian, established in 1879.

We have no advertisers. We accept no paid placements. We are not affiliated with any political party, candidate, or organization. We follow documented public records, we file FOIAs, and we cover what the other outlets don't.

Our publisher is a longtime Ypsilanti south side resident. Our research director specializes in FOIA and public records. Our work is grounded in the documented record — council minutes, court filings, IRS documents, and first-person field coverage.

The publication you're reading is a preview. The full Ypsilantian launches Tuesday, June 17, 2026 — the night of the encampment vote.
