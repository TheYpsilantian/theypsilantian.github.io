Ordinance 1465

Ypsilanti City Council · Harm Reduction Zoning Ordinance · Passed under Brown administration
What It Does

Ordinance 1465 amended Ypsilanti's zoning code to create a permitted land use category for harm reduction services — operations like LEAF Harm Reduction (211 E. Michigan Ave) that provide clean syringes, drug checking, naloxone, and related public health services. The ordinance was passed by the Ypsilanti City Council under the Brown administration and was among the most contested local policy decisions in the period covered by The Ypsilantian's archive.

Why It's Contested

Supporters of the ordinance argue harm reduction services save lives, reduce disease transmission, and connect people who use drugs to treatment — pointing to documented declines in Michigan overdose deaths. Opponents argued the ordinance enabled open drug markets near downtown businesses and residences, and that the physical conditions around Growing Hope (Amanda Edmonds' organization) and the Michigan Avenue corridor demonstrate the policy's negative ground-level effects.

The recall campaign against Desiraé Simmons in 2024 specifically cited "the Growing Hope open-drug market situation" and broader downtown conditions as grievances — linking council members who supported the harm reduction framework to observed deterioration in the downtown core.

The Policy Contradiction

Ypsilanti simultaneously holds the following policy positions in the same quarter-mile of downtown: harm reduction zoning (Ordinance 1465) permitting LEAF's operations; a $1M unarmed responder program (Care-Based Safety) that sunsetted March 2026; $750,000 in federal security funding for the Pearl Street transit center due to documented violence; and an active encampment at Water Street with an unenforceable 14-month-old vacate notice. These are not contradictions in intent — they reflect the actual complexity of governing a low-income city with high need and limited resources. But they are all happening simultaneously, and The Ypsilantian documents all of them.

Documented Connections
LEAF Harm Reduction — Primary Beneficiary LEAF (211 E. Michigan) operates under the zoning framework Ordinance 1465 created. Director Corn Williams has described LEAF as a community-facing resource open seven days a week.
Desiraé Simmons — Recall Context Simmons was recalled in part over downtown conditions linked by petitioners to the harm reduction policy framework. She survived 467–429 in May 2024 — a 38-vote margin in a 15% turnout election.
Nicole Brown — Administration Ordinance 1465 was passed under Brown's mayoralty. Brown is running for re-election in August 2026. Amber Fellows — who challenged Brown on the China trip in 2017 and is now running against her — has not made Ordinance 1465 a centerpiece of her campaign as documented in the archive.

Its March 2026 sunset — while the transit center maintains armed security and the encampment goes unenforced — is the clearest illustration of the gap between Ypsilanti's stated policy framework and its operational reality.
