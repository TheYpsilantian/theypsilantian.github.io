Brian Geiringer

A2P2 (Ann Arbor Politics and People) · Fellows Campaign · 2020 Protest Organizer · YpsiBIG Ecosystem
Who He Is

Brian Geiringer is a political operative in the Ypsilanti-Ann Arbor civic space, associated with A2P2 (Ann Arbor Politics and People) and documented as connected to Amber Fellows' 2026 mayoral campaign. He was involved in protest organizing in 2020 alongside Trische' Duckworth following the Sha'Teina Grady El incident, and operated in the YpsiBIG ecosystem — the Ypsilanti small business development network that intersected with Ann Arbor SPARK, the economic development organization Frances McMullan was emceeing events for in 2023 while under MSP investigation.

Documented Connections
Amber Fellows Campaign — 2026 Geiringer is documented in The Ypsilantian's archive as connected to the Fellows mayoral campaign. Fellows filed April 20, 2026 — one day before the deadline — to challenge Mayor Nicole Brown in the August 4 primary. Geiringer's specific role in the campaign is not fully documented in the archive.
2020 Protest Organizing — Bashert Resignation Geiringer organized during the 2020 protest period following the viral video of a Washtenaw County Sheriff's deputy punching Sha'Teina Grady El. The Workantile (WLY) account of Mayor Bashert's June 23, 2020 resignation credits Geiringer and Fellows with organizing the pressure that produced it. Trische' Duckworth organized 1,000-person protests during the same period — her role is documented separately from Geiringer and Fellows in the WLY account.
YpsiBIG / SPARK Ecosystem Geiringer operated in the YpsiBIG ecosystem — Ypsilanti's small business and economic development network that operated within Ann Arbor SPARK's orbit. SPARK is the same organization Frances McMullan was emceeing events for in 2023 while under MSP investigation for credit card theft. The connection is institutional proximity, not documented conspiracy.
A2P2 — Ann Arbor Politics and People A2P2 is a political organizing entity in the Ann Arbor-Ypsilanti corridor. Its documented activities, membership, and funding are a research direction for The Ypsilantian. Geiringer's role at A2P2 and its relationship to the broader civic network documented in this archive is not fully mapped.
What Is and Isn't Known

Geiringer appears in The Ypsilantian's archive as a political operative connected to the Fellows campaign and the 2020 organizing period — but his full role, funding sources, and institutional relationships are less thoroughly documented than the elected officials and nonprofit leaders in the network. The Ypsilantian will continue reporting on his connections as the August 4 primary approaches.
