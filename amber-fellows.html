Amber Fellows

Ward 3 Council Member, City of Ypsilanti · Mayoral Candidate, August 4, 2026 · 320 Vinewood Ct
Role & Position

Amber Fellows is the sitting Ward 3 Council Member (term runs through 2028) and the challenger to Mayor Nicole Brown in the August 4, 2026 Democratic primary. She filed on April 20, 2026 — one day before the deadline. She previously ran against Brown in 2022 and lost 76%-to-24%.

The China Trip — October 17, 2017

On October 17, 2017, Fellows stood at the public comment microphone at a Ypsilanti City Council meeting and demanded accountability for a trip to China that city officials had taken. The trip was organized through a Wayne State University student group as a pass-through, funded by developer Amy Foster. Mayor Pro-Tem Nicole Brown was a passenger. The city attorney warned in writing that the arrangement was "unethical and illegal." Fellows demanded staff be fired.

No staff were fired. No formal discipline was imposed. Brown and Fellows now serve together on the same council and have voted together on $1 million spending packages. They are now opponents in a mayoral race.

The Contradiction

Fellows demanded accountability for the China trip in 2017. She then served alongside Brown for years on council, voting together on significant spending. She is now running against Brown on an accountability platform. The question The Ypsilantian has not yet answered: what specific accountability measures does Fellows support that she has not already voted against as a council member?
