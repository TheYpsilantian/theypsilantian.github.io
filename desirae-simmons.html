Role & Background

Desiraé Simmons was elected to Ypsilanti City Council's Ward 3 seat in 2022. She serves as Mayor Pro-Tem. She is a documented member of Liberate Don't Incarcerate (LDI) — the same organization that gave Eli Savit an A rating in 2020 that helped propel him to victory over longtime prosecutor Brian Mackie. She is running for re-election to her council seat in 2026.

The Recall — 2024

In late 2023, Simmons abstained from a vote on a multi-million-dollar city building purchase intended to house a new Department of Public Services facility. The purchase passed 5-0-1 with her abstention — then was later voted down by the full council. Community members led by former Mayor Cheryl Farmer organized a recall campaign, submitting 747 signatures (exceeding the required 603) in February 2024.

The recall petition alleged Simmons was fiscally irresponsible and blamed her for businesses closing downtown, the Growing Hope open-drug market situation, and bringing Palestine issues to Ypsilanti. Simmons called the recall "anti-democratic." She survived the May 7, 2024 special election by 38 votes — 467 to 429 — in a 15% turnout race involving 897 total ballots.

Documented Connections
LDI — Liberate Don't Incarcerate Simmons is a documented LDI member. LDI gave Eli Savit an A rating in 2020 that contributed to his victory over Brian Mackie for Washtenaw County Prosecutor. Savit is now running for Michigan Attorney General while deciding whether to charge four of Sheriff Dyer's deputies in the Jenuwine shooting — 27 shots fired at an unarmed Navy veteran.
Patrick McLean — Appointment (January 2024) Simmons appointed Patrick McLean to fill the vacant Ward 2 council seat in January 2024. McLean had never previously been elected to any office. He has an Ohio area code. The August 4, 2026 primary is his first electoral race.

What She Has Said

On the recall: "I was going to be targeted no matter what. I put forward the viewpoint, perspective, and voice of people who aren't typically being heard by the council. I am very clear that I am not going to support policies that I feel are detrimental to people who have been disenfranchised."

On her recall win: "I do think that it makes clear that a lot of people in the ward are satisfied with my representation. That is vindicating."
